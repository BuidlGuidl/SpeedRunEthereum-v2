## What we're announcing

SpeedRunEthereum is launching an **AI Teaching Assistant** — a chat tutor built
directly into the site. It's live NOW and available to all registered builders.
It appears as a floating button on every challenge page; click it and a chat
panel slides in. No setup, no signup beyond the free SpeedRunEthereum account.

## Product context (for anyone unfamiliar)

SpeedRunEthereum is a free, hands-on course where developers learn to build on
Ethereum by completing a series of coding challenges (using Scaffold-ETH 2 /
Solidity). It's one of the most popular on-ramps for new Ethereum/web3 devs.
The audience: developers new to smart contracts, plus experienced devs crossing
over into web3.

## What makes the assistant different — its 4 core behaviors

(These are the exact capabilities demonstrated in our launch video.)

1. **Knows your challenge (context-aware)**
   It already knows which challenge you're on. You don't paste your code, your
   setup, or explain the task — it reads the page you're on. Ask "what am I
   building and why?" and it explains the concept in plain terms.

2. **Checks it clicked (active recall, not passive reading)**
   It doesn't just dump explanations. It offers to quiz you — "want me to test
   your understanding?" — asks a question, and confirms when you've actually
   got the concept. Learning by recall, not by skimming.

3. **Hints, not answers (it won't do the work for you)**
   If you ask it to just write the code, it refuses on purpose: "I won't hand
   you the code — you'd learn nothing." Instead it gives a targeted hint that
   points you in the right direction, then tells you to try it yourself. It's a
   teacher, not an autocomplete.

4. **Gets you running locally (real, correct setup help)**
   Ask it to help you set up, and it walks you through the actual local setup
   steps for that challenge — the real commands (create-eth, yarn chain,
   yarn deploy, yarn start across three terminals) — and offers to debug errors
   you paste in.

## Bonus: it hands off to your IDE

For deeper, code-alongside help, it points builders to open the repo in Cursor
or Claude Code and run `/start` to get a full tutor right inside their editor.

## Example (from the demo — the Crowdfunding challenge)

- Builder clicks a suggestion chip: "What am I building and why?"
- Assistant explains crowdfunding as a trust-minimized dApp (strangers pool ETH,
  the smart contract enforces the rules, refunds if the goal isn't met).
- It quizzes the builder, confirms they understood, moves on.
- Builder tries to shortcut: "just write the withdraw function for me" — the
  assistant declines and gives a hint about using a mapping instead.
- Builder asks for local setup — assistant gives the real terminal commands.
